Pew #65 is dedicated to Alexander Guy (class of 1833) and his three sons: Edward A. (‘1860), David W. (‘1862), and William Evans (‘1865).  Alexander Guy was a graduate of the [Princeton Theological Seminary](/stories/princeton-theological-seminary-and-slavery).  Born into a slave-owning family in North Carolina, he was a Presbyterian minister in Mississippi before attending medical school and relocating to Ohio.  Guy served as a director of the [American Colonization Society](/stories/princeton-and-the-colonization-movement) and donated money to seminaries for African American men and women.  His son William Evans Guy served as a Union Army surgeon during the [Civil War](/stories/princeton-and-the-civil-war).
