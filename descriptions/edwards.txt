Edwards Hall was opened in 1880 as a low-rent dormitory and extensively renovated in 1985.  The building was named for [Jonathan Edwards](/stories/jonathan-edwards), prominent 18th-century theologian and Princeton’s third president.

Jonathan Edwards (1703-58), like Princeton’s other [founders](/stories/founding-trustees) and early leaders, was an influential figure in the Great Awakening.  Although he condemned the cruelty of the slave trade and considered enslaved people his spiritual equals, Edwards purchased and owned multiple slaves over the course of his life—including an enslaved couple named Joseph and Sue, who may have [accompanied him to Princeton](/stories/presidents-house) in 1758.
