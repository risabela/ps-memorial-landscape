Jonathan Belcher’s coat of arms is carved in stone over Firestone Library’s main entrance.  Belcher (1681/2-1757) was appointed the Governor of the Royal Province of New Jersey in 1747, and issued the College of New Jersey’s second charter in 1748.  As a member of the Board of Trustees, he provided valuable support when the young college faced criticism for is commitment to non-sectarian religious education.  Belcher was among the 16 out of 23 [founding trustees](/stories/princetons-founding-trustees) who owned slaves.
